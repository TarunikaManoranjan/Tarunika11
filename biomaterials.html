Introduction

Reflex action refers to the involuntary and nearly instantaneous movement in response to a stimulus, such as touching a hot surface. This model demonstrates how reflex actions work in the human nervous system.

Working Principle

In the reflex action process, a sensory neuron sends a signal to the spinal cord, which immediately sends a signal to a motor neuron, leading to a rapid response without involving the brain. This mechanism is crucial for protecting the body from harm.
